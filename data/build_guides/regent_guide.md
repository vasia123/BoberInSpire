# Regent – Build Reference Guide
> Source: Mobalytics Slay the Spire 2 Regent Guide
> Used by: AI overlay card-pick advisor

---

## Overview

Regent has two primary archetypes built around unique mechanics: **Forging the Sovereign Blade** for a powerful win-condition weapon, and **Star generation** for explosive low-cost turns. The archetypes can partially overlap — some Star cards support Blade usage and vice versa.

---

## Archetype 1: Sovereign Blade Deck

**Win Condition:** Forge the Sovereign Blade, then use it as the primary damage dealer to delete enemies. Requires balancing Energy between playing the Blade, Forging it, and staying alive.

### Early Game Priorities (Commons)
| Card | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Cosmic Indifference | Blade recovery | One of the easiest ways to return the Blade for next turn |
| Wrought in War | Damage + Forge | Solid early damage card with built-in Forge |

### Mid Game Priorities (Uncommons)
| Card | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Bulwark | Defense + Forge | Respectable Block with a sizeable Forge amount |
| Summon Forth | Blade re-use | Quintessential Forge card — lets you re-use the Blade easily |

### High-Commitment / Payoff Cards
| Card | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Beat into Shape | Best Forge card | Especially strong alongside zero-cost attacks |
| Conqueror | Damage multiplier | Massive payoff — doubles Blade capabilities |
| Furnace | Passive Forge | Consistent low-cost Forge but requires time to build up |
| Seeking Edge | AoE solution | Solves wide combat encounters for the rest of the run |

### Relic Priorities
| Relic | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Fencing Manual | Makes the Blade more playable from Turn 1 — commit signal if found early |

### AI Pick Logic – Sovereign Blade Deck
- **Energy is the bottleneck** — always prioritize cards or relics that generate extra Energy.
- **Forge consistency first:** Summon Forth and Beat into Shape before committing to payoffs.
- **Pick Conqueror** only after reliable Forge engine is established — it's a late amplifier.
- **Include at least minor Star generation** — Falling Star and Gamma Blast apply Vulnerable on the same turn as the Blade, making them excellent combo enablers.
- **Seeking Edge** is a run-saver in multi-enemy fights — grab it if wide combats are a problem.
- **Fencing Manual** early = strong signal to commit fully to this archetype.

## Archetype 2: Star Deck

**Win Condition:** Generate Stars consistently to play powerful cards for little to no Energy. Once a reliable Star engine is online the deck becomes explosive. Slow to start — avoid over-committing early.

> **Keyword — Stars:** A resource generated by certain cards and relics. Spent to reduce or eliminate the Energy cost of powerful cards.

### Early Game Priorities (Commons)
| Card | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Gather Light | Block + Stars | Passable early Block with Star generation |
| Glow | Draw | Card draw is always welcome at any stage |
| Hidden Cache | Star generator | More commital but extremely high Stars-per-card value |
| Solar Strike | Damage + Stars | Offensive equivalent of Gather Light — fine early damage |

### Mid Game Priorities (Uncommons)
| Card | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Convergence | Hand Retain + refund | Full hand Retain can be very valuable; refunds itself next turn |
| Shining Strike | Reliable Stars | Repeated Star access for a low cost |

### High-Commitment / Payoff Cards
| Card | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Alignment | Energy conversion | Trading Stars for Energy — enables explosive turns |
| Cloak of Stars | 0-cost Block | Saves Energy for bigger combos |
| Dying Star | Multi-enemy Block | Can function as a huge Block card in multi-enemy fights |
| Gamma Blast | Vulnerable + damage | Better version of Falling Star — strong Blade crossover too |
| Genesis | Star engine | Can singlehandedly solve Star generation for some builds once online |
| Reflect | Block + counter damage | Strong Block with decent damage return |
| The Smith | Win condition | Eventual win condition even without full Blade support |

### Relic Priorities
| Relic | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| Lunar Pastry | Free passive Star generation — commit signal if found early |
| Mini Regent | Strength scaling as long as Stars are consistently spent |

### AI Pick Logic – Star Deck
- **Don't over-commit early** — pick generic cards that work even without Stars online.
- **Balance generators vs spenders** — too many pure Star generators clog the deck with "do nothing" cards.
- **Avoid Star card clustering** — Star payoff cards contest each other when drawn together; keep count reasonable.
- **Prioritize Genesis** highly once committed — it can solve generation singlehandedly.
- **Alignment** is the explosive turn enabler — pick it once Star generation is reliable.
- **Lunar Pastry or Mini Regent** early = strong signal to commit fully to Stars.
- **Gamma Blast** has crossover value in Blade decks — consider it in hybrid builds.

## AI Decision Framework

When a card pick appears after combat, the overlay AI should:

1. **Detect current archetype commitment** based on cards already in deck:
   - Summon Forth or Beat into Shape → Sovereign Blade build
   - Shining Strike, Hidden Cache, or Genesis → Star build
   - Lunar Pastry or Mini Regent relic → Star build
   - Fencing Manual relic → Blade build
   - Mixed / early game → stay generic, don't over-commit

2. **Score each offered card** against the detected archetype's priority list above.

3. **Flag crossover cards** — Gamma Blast and Falling Star are valuable in both archetypes.

4. **Warn on Star card density** — flag if deck already has many pure Star generators or Star payoff cards drawn together (they contest each other).

5. **Flag Energy gap** in Blade builds — if Energy generation is insufficient, deprioritize costly Blade plays and highlight Energy-producing options.

6. **Flag Vulnerable sources** — Blade builds need at least one reliable Vulnerable applicator (Gamma Blast, Falling Star) for optimal output.
